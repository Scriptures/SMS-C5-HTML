COMMITMENT TO SERVE

Introduction

Commitment to serve does not arise out of nowhere. It comes from and is guided by values, and assumptions that spring from our
backgrounds, cultures, experiences and from our sentiment about what is right. These values shapes our vision of the world as it
should be, and motivates us to try to make it so.

Background

Growing up from a home where financial challenges determined the future of its young ones was not an easy burden to live with.
My mother often reminded me of how difficult it is for minorities like us to earn respect from society and enough money to raise
a family. She encouraged me to strive for the best by being selfish and competitive against my peers rather than to enjoy what I like;
which was to interact with people. She always wanted me to become a medical doctor saying it is a well-paying job. Though that was
not my career goal, yet I was not ready to ignore her concern since she played the role of both parents due to the absence of my father,
and her decision where not to be questioned.

In secondary school, I had to battle between fulfilling the desires of my mother and my aspirations in life. Though the desires of
my family weighed more than my personal goals, and that I pursued a course which carries me a step closer to achieving the goals
of my mother for me, I still believed my career goal as a Software Engineer was already latent. The passion of becoming
who I wanted to become, conflicted with my decision and this carried me to a point where I needed to choose between my future
and my obligations as a son.

This whole experience helped in fortifying my emotional intelligence and also improved, causing me to thinking beyond what is obvious
and in creating suitable alternatives with extremely limited resources, thus, reinforcing my belief to listen to my inner voice and do
what is ethically right. It taught me to deal with emotional barriers at school and anywhere I found myself among people;
I was glad that I rose above those barriers. Also, this experience taught me to be logical and patient when dealing with sensitive issues.

Conclusion

In conclusion, the maturity gained from this experience has not just boosted my confidence, but has caused me to realise how influential
personal goals are to the individual future. One of my personal goals is to study in a community with adequate academic resources,
leadership training, practical approach to learning, brilliant faculty with diverse professional background and perfect location,
and it is possible that Azubi Africa Frontend Development program has the critical tools and network needed to
achieve my career aspirations.
